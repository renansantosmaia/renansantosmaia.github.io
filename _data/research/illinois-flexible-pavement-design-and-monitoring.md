---
title: Illinois Flexible Pavement Design and Monitoring
client: Illinois Department of Transportation (Project R27-267)
period: 2025–Present
type: Research Project
summary: "Current project focused on updating IDOT’s pavement design procedures
  by reviewing 20+ years of extended-life projects, and evaluating emerging
  factors such as climate change, truck platooning, and increased axle loads.
  This project encompasses field and laboratory analysis of AC mixes used for
  perpetual pavement configurations, including fracture and fatigue tests,
  network-level evaluation using Traffic Speed Deflectometer, characterization
  of multiscale mechanical responses under realistic testing configurations
  (waveforms and compaction conditions), elastic recovery and long-term healing
  potential, as well as the effect of saturation on moisture-damage potential.
  PIs: Ramez Hajj and Marshall Thompson."
