About

Research-to-Practice
Engineer

I connect laboratory rigor to field reality — and field reality to the decisions that shape infrastructure networks.

I am a PhD Candidate in Civil Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Illinois Center for Transportation. My doctoral research bridges flexible pavement material behavior and structural response to inform agency-level decision-making — integrating laboratory characterization, field evaluation, mechanistic modeling, and network-scale management.

My professional trajectory spans over a decade across research, field, and industry environments in Brazil, the United Kingdom, and the United States. I have led applied research programs for IDOT, worked as a Civil Engineer on Brazil's Transnordestina Railway, and held field and plant roles in asphalt construction and airport pavement QA/QC.

I believe technical rigor is not the opposite of innovation — it is its precondition. The most consequential improvements to engineering practice come from understanding, with depth and honesty, why current solutions perform the way they do and where they fall short.

Personal

Beyond the Lab
& the Office

I am Brazilian. I have lived in the UK and the American Midwest. Moving between cultures is one of the best engineering educations I have had.

It teaches you to listen carefully, communicate across difference, and find common ground quickly — skills I consider just as critical in engineering as any technical method. Every project involves people with different priorities, backgrounds, and pressures. The ability to navigate that is not soft; it is structural.

My teaching experience reinforced this. Making complex mechanics intuitive to skeptical undergraduates trains a communicative patience that carries directly into project teams, client meetings, and field coordination. I am the kind of engineer who finds the solution with the team, not around it.

I thrive in dynamic environments. I am fully open to relocating and drawn to solving complex infrastructure challenges — whether constrained urban sites, legacy systems, or geotechnical problems in rural or industrial contexts. I value environments where technical rigor meets real-world constraints and where solutions must be simultaneously deployable and robust.

Outside of work, I am energized by sports, fitness, and people. I work out regularly and love lifting — discipline and consistency in the gym translate directly to how I approach engineering problems. As a former high school volleyball athlete, I still carry that competitive spirit and team mentality. I am passionate about socializing, spending time with friends, and sharing good moments. Music and exploring cities on foot are how I recharge — and yes, walking through cities is an excellent way to develop opinions about pavement quality.
