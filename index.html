About

Research-to-Practice
Engineer

I connect laboratory rigor to field reality — and field reality to the decisions that shape infrastructure networks.

I am a PhD Candidate in Civil Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Illinois Center for Transportation. My doctoral research bridges flexible pavement material behavior and structural response to inform agency-level decision-making — integrating laboratory characterization, field evaluation, mechanistic modeling, and network-scale management.

My professional trajectory spans over a decade across research, field, and industry environments in Brazil, the United Kingdom, and the United States. I have led applied research programs for IDOT, worked as a Civil Engineer on Brazil's Transnordestina Railway, and held field and plant roles in asphalt construction and airport pavement QA/QC.

I believe technical rigor is not the opposite of innovation — it is its precondition. The most consequential improvements to engineering practice do not come from replicating what is standard, but from understanding, with depth and honesty, why current solutions perform the way they do and where they fall short.

Illinois Flexible Pavement Design & Monitoring

Personal

Beyond the Lab
& the Office

I am Brazilian. I have lived in the UK and spent the last several years in the American Midwest. Moving between cultures is one of the best engineering educations I have had.

It teaches you to listen carefully, communicate across difference, and find common ground quickly — skills I consider just as critical in engineering as any technical method. Every project involves people with different priorities, backgrounds, and pressures. The ability to navigate that is not soft; it is structural.

My teaching experience has reinforced this. Standing in front of a classroom and making complex mechanics intuitive to undergraduates who are simultaneously overwhelmed and skeptical trains a kind of communicative patience that carries directly into project teams, client meetings, and field coordination. I am the kind of engineer who finds the solution with the team, not around it.

I thrive in dynamic, high-energy environments. Big cities energize me — the density, the complexity, the pace. I am fully open to relocating, and I am drawn to the kind of problems that only dense urban infrastructure creates: constrained sites, competing stakeholder interests, legacy systems, and the need for solutions that are simultaneously technically rigorous and practically deployable.

Outside of work, I run long distances, which has taught me more about pacing, endurance, and honest self-assessment than almost anything else. I explore cities on foot whenever I can, which — it turns out — is an excellent way to develop opinions about pavement quality.
